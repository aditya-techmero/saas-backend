Cats in Hindu Scriptures

Contrary to popular belief, cats aren't typically revered in Hindu mythology as they are in certain other cultures. Nevertheless, there are specific references to cats in various Hindu scriptures that shed light on their symbolic roles and connections to the divine.

  • Shashthi: In some texts, the goddess Shashthi — the deity of fertility and children — is depicted riding a cat. Shashthi's association with cats underscores the protection and nurturing aspects attributed to the goddess.
  • Legend of the Vanara and the Cat: This tale from the epic Ramayana offers a cautionary tale about the deceptive appearances of certain beings. A vanara (monkey-like humanoid) is tricked by a cat pretending to be a devotee of Lord Rama, revealing the cat's symbolic association with trickery and illusion.
  • The Cat in the Mahabharata: In another story from the great Indian epic Mahabharata, a cat deceives a mouse by pretending to be a yogi, demonstrating the cat's association with cunning and deceit.

The Connection Between Cats and Hindu Gods

While cats are not directly worshipped as deities in Hinduism, they are associated with certain gods and goddesses:

  • Durga: The warrior goddess Durga is often associated with lions, not domestic cats. However, as a divine feline, the lion shares with cats the characteristics of strength, courage, and fierce independence.
  • Lakshmi: Some believe that the goddess Lakshmi, deity of wealth and prosperity, once took the form of a cat during the churning of the ocean (Samudra Manthan) in Hindu mythology.

Notable Feline Deities in Hinduism

Hindu mythology is replete with deities that have feline attributes or forms. Some of the most prominent ones include:

  • Durga: Known as the invincible goddess, Durga is often depicted riding a lion or a tiger. Her feline mount represents power, will, and determination, and she is revered for her fearless nature and her role as a protective mother figure.
  • Shasti: Shasti is the goddess of childbirth and fertility, often depicted riding a cat. She is revered by those seeking the blessing of progeny and her cat symbolizes watchfulness and protection.
  • Narasimha: An avatar of Lord Vishnu, Narasimha has the head of a lion and the body of a man. His form represents the divine's ability to take on any form, and his fierce nature signifies the protective and righteous aspect of divinity.

Mythological Stories Featuring Divine Felines

Several stories in Hindu mythology highlight the role and significance of divine felines.

  • Durga's Battle with Mahishasura: In this tale, Durga, astride a lion, battles the buffalo demon, Mahishasura. The story symbolizes the triumph of good over evil and the power of divine femininity.
  • Narasimha and Hiranyakashipu: This story tells of Narasimha's protection of his devotee, Prahlada, from his tyrant father, Hiranyakashipu. The lion-man form of Narasimha embodies the divine's unflinching protection of devotees.
  • The Tale of Shasti and the Seven Little Mice: In a charming tale, Shasti saves seven little mice from a cruel elephant. The story underscores the goddess's role as a protector and nurturer.
